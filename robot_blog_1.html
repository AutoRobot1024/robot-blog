Robot learning

Robot learning combine machine learning with robot control, algorithms enable robots to autonomously learn new skills and interact with the physical environment. You can refer to the news on Robotic Learning in the IEEE section: https://www.ieee-ras.org/robot-learning/news

Embodied AI

Embodied AI intelligence, with the rapid development of LLM which integrates and combines into an extension of the new generation of wearable devices, Robotics and Agents. It also enhances the ability to interact with devices such as voice/gestures, and can further focus on application level research and development. This concept can be referenced from the most popular ones currently available: https://research.nvidia.com/labs/gear/

Collaborative robots educational robots service robots

Research in the fields of collaborative robots, educational robots, and service robots is hot. This is thanks to the emergence and promotion of OpenAi, represented by ChatGPT. The combination of big language models and segmented industries has just begun to emerge, and the market is hot but talent is scarce, which is a direction.

L-SLAM/V-SLAM Navigation

Intelligent driving, AMR mobile robots, service robots, humanoid robots, and other core technologies related to navigation and path planning that the robotics industry continues to focus on. Generally speaking, these algorithms and techniques are combined with ROS. The most popular event of this technology currently is Wuhan Radish Travel, where car sensors are blocked by plastic bags and the car cannot operate. SLAM is currently and will remain a core technology hotspot for a long time to come.

Visual recognition algorithm and radar recognition algorithm

This is the first level core of the robot navigation algorithm. Different algorithms are closely related to different sensor devices and belong to the fundamental scientific nature of the robotics industry. These types of talents have a wide range of employment opportunities, not just robots.

Sensor

Lidar, binocular cameras, depth cameras, ultrasound, TOF sensors, and other important technological cores for robot embodied intelligence. This is a more fundamental and easily disruptive part of the robotics technology ecosystem. At present, almost all the good sensors in the industry are foreign brands. In recent years, the trend of sensor localization has been tight, and many companies have also risen rapidly.

Battery

This is not only the robotics industry, but also an unavoidable technological bottleneck for devices that use energy storage.

Advanced control algorithm adaptive control

an adaptive control method for addressing the high nonlinearity and complex dynamic characteristics of parallel robots. Robust control: Improve the stability and performance of robots in uncertain environments. Reinforcement learning and deep learning control: Combining artificial intelligence technology to optimize robot control strategies, especially for autonomous decision-making in unknown or complex environments.

Optimization and Dynamics Modeling Dynamics Modeling and Simulation

The establishment and validation of high-precision dynamics models provide a theoretical basis for control algorithms and motion planning. Structural optimization: Lightweight design and stiffness optimization of parallel robot mechanisms to improve performance and efficiency. Real time dynamic calculation: a method of accelerating dynamic calculation to ensure the response speed of parallel robots in real-time operation.

Multi sensor fusion of sensing and perception

By combining various sensor information such as vision, force perception, and touch, more accurate environmental perception and operation can be achieved. Force feedback control: using force sensors for precise force control, applied to fine assembly and micro operations.

Human computer collaboration, safety, and interaction

Research on the safety and natural interaction methods of parallel robots in collaboration with humans, including collision detection and avoidance, human-machine integration control, etc. Wearable robots: The application of parallel robots in wearable devices, such as rehabilitation assisted exoskeletons.

Design of Flexible Materials and Mechanisms for Flexible Robots

Study the flexible structure of parallel robots and use flexible materials to improve adaptability and safety. Soft robot: Combining soft robot technology, design a new type of parallel mechanism for complex environmental operations.

Application Fields: Medical Machines

The application of parallel robots in medical fields such as surgery and rehabilitation, especially in high-precision and minimally invasive operations. Industrial automation: The application of parallel robots in industrial automation such as high-precision assembly and rapid handling. Space robots: The application of parallel robots in space exploration, such as space station maintenance, satellite repair, etc.
