The M4 Morphobot

quipped with NVIDIA Jetson platform, released by the California Institute of Technology in the United States, is one of them. This "versatile" robot can not only fly, travel, and walk, but also complete eight sets of arranged and combined movements through combination skills, known as the "Multi Modal Mobility Morphobot" (M4 for short). This transformable robot is designed to meet the task requirements of different fields, such as search and rescue. In hazardous areas such as fires, M4 can fly into the air for reconnaissance and evaluate the situation through cameras and sensors. Emergency personnel such as the fire department believe that they can provide important support in areas that cannot be accessed by driving or walking. Compared to traditional drones, M4 can land in places far away from crowds and then travel to the destination to complete delivery, making it safer and more reliable. M4 can fly to areas that are difficult for delivery trucks to enter, becoming an innovative solution to the safety and feasibility issues faced by drone delivery.
